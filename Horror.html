"Small Spaces" by Katherine Arden is a gripping middle-grade horror novel. When Ollie discovers a mysterious book about sinister folk tales, her class field trip turns into a chilling adventure. Confronting a dark force and facing her fears, Ollie learns the importance of courage and friendship in this eerie and atmospheric tale.

"The Graveyard Book" by Neil Gaiman follows Nobody Owens, raised by ghosts in a cemetery after his family's murder. As he navigates the supernatural world and evades a killer, he discovers his connection to both the living and the dead. Gaiman weaves a captivating and darkly whimsical coming-of-age tale.

"Coraline" by Neil Gaiman tells the story of a young girl who discovers a parallel world with alternate, button-eyed versions of her parents. Initially enchanted, Coraline soon realizes the dark secrets lurking in this other realm. Gaiman's masterful storytelling weaves a creepy and enchanting narrative, exploring bravery and the power of imagination.

"The Night Gardener" by Jonathan Auxier is a middle-grade novel that follows Irish orphans Molly and Kip as they work for the Windsor family. Set in an eerie mansion, the story unfolds with mystery and dark enchantment, exploring themes of fear, courage, and the consequences of unchecked desire.

"Nightbooks" by J.A. White is a middle-grade fantasy-horror novel that follows Alex, a boy with a penchant for writing scary stories. When he is trapped by a witch in an apartment filled with frightening tales, Alex must use his storytelling skills to survive. The book explores themes of creativity, bravery, and friendship.

"There's Someone Inside Your House" is a gripping thriller by Stephanie Perkins. In a small town, students are targeted by a masked killer revealing their darkest secrets. As the body count rises, the survivors must confront their own fears and unravel the mystery before becoming the next victim in this heart-pounding tale.

"The Girl from the Well" by Rin Chupeco is a haunting tale inspired by Japanese folklore. Okiku, a vengeful spirit, roams the world, punishing child murderers. When she encounters a teenage boy with a dark secret, their lives intertwine, revealing a chilling and atmospheric story of revenge, spirits, and the supernatural.

"The Diviners" by Libba Bray is a captivating paranormal thriller set in 1920s New York. Evie O'Neill, possessing a supernatural gift, joins a group of gifted individuals to solve a series of occult murders. As they unravel a sinister mystery, dark forces and hidden powers emerge, threatening the city's very existence.

"Rot & Ruin" by Jonathan Maberry unfolds in a post-apocalyptic world overrun by zombies. Benny Imura, a teenager, reluctantly becomes a zombie hunter under the guidance of his older brother. As they navigate the dangerous wasteland, the novel explores themes of survival, morality, and the complexities of human nature in the face of a zombie-infested reality.

"The Monstrumologist" by Rick Yancey is a chilling historical horror novel. Dr. Warthrop, a monstrumologist, and his young apprentice, Will Henry, investigate gruesome supernatural phenomena in 19th-century New England. As they confront nightmarish creatures, the story delves into the dark and macabre, blending horror with a tale of loyalty and the pursuit of knowledge.
