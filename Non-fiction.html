"The Alchemist" by Paulo Coelho follows Santiago, a shepherd, on a journey to discover his personal legend. Guided by omens and the pursuit of his dreams, Santiago encounters mystical forces, learns life lessons, and discovers the true meaning of treasure. This philosophical novel explores destiny, spirituality, and the pursuit of one's dreams.

"I Am Malala" by Malala Yousafzai, co-written with Christina Lamb, recounts the inspiring true story of Malala's fight for girls' education in Pakistan. Shot by the Taliban at a young age, Malala's resilience and advocacy led her to become a global symbol for education and women's rights, earning her the Nobel Peace Prize.

"What If?: Serious Scientific Answers to Absurd Hypothetical Questions" by Randall Munroe is a humorous exploration of bizarre and imaginative scenarios using scientific principles. The book, written by the creator of the webcomic XKCD, provides witty and entertaining answers to questions like "What would happen if you tried to hit a baseball pitched at 90% the speed of light?" blending science and humor in an engaging way.

"Bomb" by Steve Sheinkin chronicles the suspenseful race to build the atomic bomb during World War II. The narrative unveils the scientific and espionage efforts of the Manhattan Project, unraveling the high-stakes race between the United States and Nazi Germany to possess the world's most formidable weapon, shaping the course of history.

In "The Boy Who Harnessed the Wind," William Kamkwamba, a young Malawian, overcomes famine by building a windmill. Self-taught, he harnesses wind energy, bringing electricity to his village. His story of innovation and resilience illustrates the transformative power of one person's determination to create positive change in the face of adversity.

"Flowers in the Gutter" tells the true story of Edelweiss Pirates, rebellious German teens who resisted the Nazis in World War II. This inspiring account highlights their acts of defiance, promoting individuality and cultural resistance against oppressive forces.

"The Family Romanov" by Candace Fleming recounts the tragic tale of Russia's last royal family, the Romanovs. Exploring their lives amidst political upheaval, the book details the Russian Revolution and the execution of Tsar Nicholas II and his family, offering a poignant glimpse into the human impact of historical change.

"Honor Girl" by Maggie Thrash is a graphic memoir capturing the author's coming-of-age at an all-girls' summer camp. It explores themes of identity and teenage relationships, providing an intimate account of Maggie's self-discovery amid the challenges of adolescence and the complexities of her feelings in a compelling visual narrative..

"(Don't) Call Me Crazy: 33 Voices Start the Conversation about Mental Health" is an anthology edited by Kelly Jensen. Featuring diverse perspectives, this collection offers insightful essays, art, and narratives, initiating a vital conversation about mental health. The contributors share personal experiences, fostering understanding and challenging stigmas surrounding mental health.

"Bonnie and Clyde: The Making of a Legend" by Karen Blumenthal delves into the true story of the infamous criminal duo, exploring the social context that shaped Bonnie Parker and Clyde Barrow. This insightful account combines historical details and analysis to provide a nuanced understanding of their lives and criminal legacy.
