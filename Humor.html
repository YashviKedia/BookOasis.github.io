"The Summer I Robbed a Bank" by David O'Doherty and Chris Judge recounts the hilarious escapades of a group of friends who, armed with a sofa, embark on an unlikely bank heist. This whimsical adventure is filled with humor, friendship, and unexpected twists, making it a delightful and entertaining read.

"Little Badman and the Time-travelling Teacher of Doom" unfolds comical adventures as a time-traveling teacher disrupts school, sparking chaos. Little Badman and friends navigate the mayhem, blending humor and imagination in this delightful children's tale, creating a whimsical journey filled with laughter and unexpected twists that captivates young readers.

"I Am a Genius of Unspeakable Evil and I Want to Be Your Class President" by Josh Lieb follows Oliver Watson, a seemingly ordinary middle-schooler who secretly orchestrates global schemes from behind the scenes. As he runs for class president, Oliver's comedic and cunning endeavors provide a humorous exploration of power, identity, and the complexities of adolescence.

"The Extremely Embarrassing Life of Lottie Brooks" unfolds the humorous misadventures of teenager Lottie. Navigating awkward moments, crushes, and family quirks, Lottie's escapades offer a lighthearted exploration of adolescence, self-discovery, and the resilience to embrace one's imperfections in this entertaining and relatable coming-of-age story.

"The Worst Class Trip Ever" by Dave Barry is a hilarious middle-grade novel following Wyatt Palmer and his classmates on a chaotic trip to Washington, D.C. Filled with witty humor and unexpected twists, the story turns an ordinary school excursion into a memorable adventure of mistaken identities and secret agent shenanigans.

"Me and Earl and the Dying Girl" by Jesse Andrews follows the story of Greg Gaines, who reluctantly befriends a classmate diagnosed with cancer. Greg, along with his friend Earl, attempts to navigate the complexities of high school and friendship in this poignant and humorous tale, exploring themes of life, death, and connection.

In "Boys Don't Knit (In Public)" by T.S. Easton, teenage troublemaker Ben Fletcher is sentenced to community service, leading him to the unexpected world of knitting. Amidst hilarious mishaps, Ben navigates the challenges of unraveling yarn and discovers the therapeutic power of creativity, friendship, and personal growth.

In "Dan Versus Nature" by Don Calame, reluctant teenager Dan is coerced into a wilderness therapy trip with his stepfather and a group of quirky teens. As they face wild challenges and comedic mishaps, Dan's journey becomes a hilarious exploration of nature, friendship, and self-discovery in this entertaining coming-of-age novel.

"Beauty Queens" by Libba Bray is a satirical novel that follows a group of beauty pageant contestants stranded on a deserted island after a plane crash. As they face survival challenges, the diverse group of girls questions societal expectations and explores themes of identity, feminism, and the absurdity of beauty standards.

"The Perks of Being a Wallflower" by Stephen Chbosky is an intimate coming-of-age novel narrated through letters. Shy and observant Charlie navigates the complexities of high school, forming deep friendships with two seniors, Sam and Patrick. The story delves into the joys, heartaches, and transformative experiences of adolescence, capturing its raw essence.
